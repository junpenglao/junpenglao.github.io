Most personal sites present knowledge as a finished artifact: a polished list of
papers, a tidy bio, a portfolio frozen at the moment of publishing. This one is
trying something a little different. It treats knowledge as a **process** rather
than a product — and it tries to make that process visible on the page.

## Living documents

Every piece of writing here is a living document. Each one carries two small
markers in its header:

- a **status** — `draft`, `working`, or `stable` — that tells you how settled an
  idea is; and
- a **last-updated** date, so you can see when I last touched it.

A `draft` is a thought still finding its shape. It is reachable if you have the
link, but it is kept off the public [Writing](/writing/) list and marked
`noindex`, because publishing is not the same as finishing. A `working`
document is out in the open but still moving. A `stable` one is settled enough
that I would stand behind it today — which is not the same as forever.

This is deliberate. The convention is borrowed from how I actually work: ideas
get version numbers, claims come with caveats, and revising in public is treated
as a feature, not an embarrassment.

## Cross-links and caveats

Ideas here are meant to connect. Where a claim leans on another note, it links
to it; where I am unsure, I say so, in the text or in a margin note rather than
quietly omitting the doubt. Negative results and abandoned approaches are worth
recording too — the path you did **not** take is part of what you learned.

## The design

The look follows the Swiss International Typographic tradition: a strong grid,
flush-left text, generous whitespace, large readable type, and a single
restrained accent. The aim is to get out of the way of the reading. The
typeface is [Hanken Grotesk](https://hanken.co/products/hanken-grotesk),
self-hosted; there are no third-party embeds, no tracking scripts, and almost no
client-side JavaScript. The site is statically built and meant to load fast and
stay legible for a long time.

## Why "knowledge as process"

The deeper reason for all of this is a bet I am still working out: that the most
valuable part of thinking is often the *trace* — the corrections, the dead ends,
the reasons a first attempt was wrong — and not just the clean result at the end.
A site that hides its revisions throws that trace away. This one tries to keep
some of it.

More to come. Consider everything here a work in progress, in the best sense.
